# Fairness, Bias, and Ethics in ML

## Why this matters
ML systems can affect large numbers of people, so fairness and harm reduction matter.

## Common problems
Examples of harmful or biased systems:
- hiring tools that discriminate
- face recognition systems with racial bias
- loan models that treat subgroups unfairly
- systems that reinforce stereotypes

## Misuse and harmful applications
ML can also be used for:
- deepfakes without consent
- toxic or misleading content
- fake comments or bots
- fraud
- spam and other abusive systems

## Practical guidance

### 1. Build a diverse team
Diverse teams are better at identifying possible harms.

### 2. Check standards and guidelines
Look for industry, legal, or domain-specific fairness guidance.

### 3. Audit the system
Test the model for performance gaps across subgroups.

### 4. Prepare a mitigation plan
Have a rollback or response plan ready before deployment.

### 5. Monitor after deployment
Continue checking for harm, drift, or bias after launch.

## Key takeaway
Do not treat fairness as an afterthought.  
Think about harm early, audit before deployment, and monitor after deployment.
